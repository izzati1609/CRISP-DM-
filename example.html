Project: Retail Sales Forecasting

Scenario: Supermarket chain "FreshMart" Inventory Optimization

Problem: A supermarket chain suffers from stockouts on weekends. They need to predict exactly how many units of milk and bread to order for the next 7 days.

Phase 1: Business Understanding

Goal: Reduce "Out of Stock" events by 20% without increasing waste.

Problem: Current ordering is done by "gut feeling" (agak-agak), leading to lost sales on Saturdays.

Phase 2: Data Understanding

We gathered 2 years of historical sales data from the Point of Sales (POS) system. Here is a snapshot of the Raw Data:

Phase 3: Data Preparation

Before modeling, we had to clean the data:

  • Handling Missing Values: We filled empty 'CompetitionDistance' cells with the median value.
  • Feature Engineering: We extracted "Day of Week" (Mon, Tue, Wed) from the Date column.
  • Filtering: We removed rows where Open = 0 (Store Closed) because 0 sales is not useful for prediction.

Phase 4: Modeling

We chose the Random Forest Regressor algorithm.

Why? Because retail data is non-linear (sales spike suddenly on Payday or Christmas). Linear Regression is too simple to capture these patterns.

Phase 5: Evaluation

We compared our model's prediction (Blue) against the actual sales (Red) for the test week.

Result: The model follows the actual trend closely (92% Accuracy).

Phase 6: Deployment

The model is now "Live". Every Sunday night, it automatically runs and emails the Store Manager a Suggested Order List for the week.

Business Impact: Stockouts reduced by 18% in the first month.
